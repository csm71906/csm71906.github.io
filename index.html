Tiger Woods: The Greatest Golfer of All-Time

Tiger's Childhood

Eldrick Woods, known to millions of fans as Tiger Woods, was born as an only child on December 30, 1975, in Cypress, California. His father, Earl Woods, recognized his talent from a young age and from there devoted a lot of his time to ensure that his son's talents could flourish. Tiger won his first tournament at the age of 8, and from that point on, there was no limit to how good he would become. There is no doubt that golf was a major part of Tiger's life, especially when he spent countless hours practicing his swing and techniques, but this did not keep him from living a normal childhood. Tiger still joked around and ate sweets as a normal child would do, however none of us could quite expect what would be in store for young Tiger Wood's future.

Tiger's Junior and College Career

Tiger Woods was a dominant force no one had ever seen before on the junior and collegiate golf circuit. Woods won the U.S. Junior Championship an unpresidented 3 times in a row and in 1994 became the youngest winner in the tournament's history. He also won countless other tournaments on various top level junior golf tours, which concluded one of the best junior golf careers ever seen.


Tiger continued his dominant golf career by enrolling at Stanford University in the fall of 1994 on a full ride golf scholarship. While in college at Stanford he won numerous college tournaments highlighted by an NCAA individual championshipn in 1996. While in college, his amatuer career did not seem to skip a beat either. He continued his domination of USGA Championships by winning the U.S. Amateur Championship three years straight in 1994, 1995, and 1996. Seeing his career truly beginning to flourish, Tiger forewent his junior and senior year of college at Stanford in order to become a professional golfer.

Tiger's Professional Golf Career

Professional Career Highlights

  • 79 official PGA Tour victories
  • 11 time PGA Tour Player of the Year
  • 10 time PGA Tour Money Leader
  • Lowest career scoring average in PGA Tour history

Tiger Woods turned professional in August of 1996 and looked to have a smooth transition from his amateur career to his life on the PGA Tour. Woods began his career by signing endoresement deals with Nke and Titleist that were two of the highest paying endoresement deals the golf world had ever seen. He recorded his first victory on the PGA Tour at the Las Vegas Invitational, which was a mere 2 months into Wood's professional career. Tiger went on to win one more time in the 1996 season before being named PGA Tour Rookie of the Year and earning a spot in the Tour Championship. Tiger Woods was quickly becoming a golf legacy that everyone loved and wanted to follow around at every tournament he played in. Five out of the next seven years Woods had at least 5 victories on the PGA Tour and was now the face of Nike Golf. Woods currently has 79 career wins, which is second all-time among golfers, but perhaps the single most impressive thing he accomplished in his professional golf career were the 14 major golf championships he won, second all-time to only Jack Nicklaus. Though he continues to chase Jack's mark to add to his legacy, there is no doubt Tiger Woods will go down in history as the greatest golfer of all-time for everything he has done, on and off the course, for the game of golf.
